Recommended Equipment

High-leverage, low-profile gear. Total investment: ~$100–$180. Fits in a backpack.

  • 1
    Gymnastics Rings
    The single most versatile piece of equipment. Replaces cable rows, dips, push-ups with infinitely adjustable difficulty. Hang from any bar, tree, beam. Enables rows, ring push-ups, dips, support holds, L-sit, and eventually muscle-ups.
    $25–$50 · Wood rings, 1.5" straps · ~1.5 lbs
  • 2
    Pull-Up Bar (Portable/Doorframe)
    You already do 5×5 pull-ups — this keeps that movement alive. Pairs with rings for rows. Look for one that can also be used on a door or mounted over a ledge when traveling.
    $25–$50 doorframe · or use any bar/beam at hotels/parks
  • 3
    Push-Up Bars (Rotating)
    Protects wrists during high-volume push-up work. Allows deeper ROM than floor push-ups. Rotating handles let you find neutral wrist position. Pairs with ring push-ups for a full push system.
    $15–$25 · ~0.8 lbs · compact
  • 4
    Mini Resistance Bands (set of 3)
    Face pulls for shoulder health, band-assisted pull-ups when fatigued, hip activation before lower body work, banded push-ups for extra challenge, lateral band walks. Incredibly versatile for under $20.
    $15–$20 · ~0.3 lbs · fits in any pocket
  • 5
    Ab Wheel (optional)
    One of the most brutal core exercises per unit of time. Directly trains anti-extension — important for hiking stability and injury prevention. Very lightweight and compact.
    $12–$20 · ~0.6 lbs

12-Week Program — Overcoming Gravity Phases
OG Progression Rule: You advance to the next harder variation only when you can hit 3 sets of 5 clean reps (strength work) or 3 sets of 8 clean reps (hypertrophy work) at your current level. Quality over speed — always. Tendons adapt 3–10× slower than muscles. Earn each progression.
Weeks 1–4 · OG Foundation
Accumulate
Build quality volume at your current level in all 6 movement patterns. Establish the OG session structure: skill → strength → accessory. Learn hollow body, L-sit holds, and ring row positioning. Zone 2 aerobic base (3×/week).
REP TARGET THIS PHASE
Primary: 3–4 × 5–8 reps  ·  Accessory: 3 × 10–15 reps
Hollow Body Ring Rows Split Squat
Weeks 5–8 · OG Progress
Advance
Move up one level in each pattern where you've hit the rep standard. Introduce ring dips, elevated pike push-ups, box pistols, Nordic negatives, L-sit on bars. Add one threshold run per week.
REP TARGET THIS PHASE
Primary: 4 × 5–8 reps  ·  Add harder variations at 3×3–5 first
Ring Dips Wall HSPU Box Pistol
Weeks 9–12 · OG Peak
Consolidate
Advance to highest earned level. Full wall HSPU, archer rows, L-sit on rings, full pistol squats, partial Nordic curls. Build volume at peak levels. Adventure-ready fitness — all-day hiking, cycling, swimming, climbing.
REP TARGET THIS PHASE
Primary: 4–5 × 5–8 reps  ·  Volume: 25–50 reps/pattern/session
HSPU Full Pistol L-sit Rings

OG Session Order: Skill Work → Primary Strength → Secondary Strength → Hypertrophy/Accessory → Prehab. Always in this order. Skill/technique work needs a fresh nervous system — never put it after heavy lifting.

OG NOTES / ADVANCE WHEN
SKILL: Wall Handstand Hold
V-Push skill — CNS fresh, do first
3 × 20–60s
Kick up to wall, straight body, shoulders shrugged into ears. Not a push — just holding. Builds proprioception for eventual HSPU. Rest 90s between holds.
💪 STRENGTH A: H-Push Progression
Ring push-up → Archer push-up → Ring dip
4 × 5–8 reps
Advance when: 3×8 at current level. Wk1-4: Ring push-ups (feet on floor). Wk5-8: Archer push-ups. Wk9+: Ring dips. 2–3 min rest between sets.
💪 STRENGTH B: V-Push Progression
Pike push-up → Elevated pike → Wall HSPU neg
3 × 5–8 reps
Wk1-4: Elevated pike push-up (feet on chair). Wk5-8: Wall HSPU negatives (5s lower). Wk9+: Full wall HSPU. 2 min rest. This is your OHP replacement.
📦 ACCESSORY: Push-up Volume
Sub-maximal, hypertrophy rep range
3 × 10–15 reps
Standard or decline push-ups. These are your volume work — should feel moderate. Focus on full ROM and a 2-second pause at bottom. 60–90s rest.
🔵 CORE: L-Sit Progression
Compression strength — all skills need this
3 × 10–20s holds
Start: floor L-sit (tuck). Progress: push-up bars → rings. Advance when you hit 3×20s. This is OG's fundamental core builder — more useful than any crunch.
🩺 PREHAB: Band Face Pulls + Scapular Push-ups
Non-negotiable shoulder health
3 × 15–20 reps
Face pulls: anchor band at eye height, pull thumbs to ears. Scapular push-ups: protract/retract at bottom of plank. OG emphasizes this prevents most shoulder issues.

▼

OG NOTES / ADVANCE WHEN
SKILL: Dead Hang + Scapular Pulls
Shoulder prep and straight-arm strength
3 × 30–60s hang + 10 scap pulls
Dead hang: full passive hang, relax everything. Scapular pull: from dead hang, depress shoulders without bending elbows — this activates the lats before strength work. Essential OG warm-up.
💪 STRENGTH A: V-Pull Progression
Pull-up → Close-grip → L-sit pull-up → Archer
4 × 5–8 reps
You start at close-grip pull-up. Advance when: 3×8. Wk1-4: Vary grip (overhand, close, wide). Wk5-8: L-sit pull-up (tuck first). Wk9+: Archer pull-up or weighted. 2–3 min rest.
💪 STRENGTH B: H-Pull Progression
Ring row → elevated feet → archer row
3 × 8–12 reps
Wk1-4: Horizontal ring row (body parallel to floor). Wk5-8: Feet elevated ring row. Wk9+: Archer ring row (one-arm emphasis). Advance when: 3×12 clean. 90s rest.
📦 ACCESSORY: Ring / Band Curls
Bicep hypertrophy, elbow health
3 × 10–15 reps
Underhand ring rows with a short ROM (curl emphasis), or band curls. OG notes bicep work protects elbows under heavy pull loads. 60s rest, moderate effort.
📦 ACCESSORY: Band Pull-Aparts
Rear delt / horizontal scapular strength
3 × 15–20 reps
Band held at chest, pull apart to T position. Crucial for posture and rear delt balance against all the push work. Do these daily if possible — they're nearly impossible to overdo.
🩺 PREHAB: Dislocates + YTW
Shoulder mobility and cuff health
2 × 10 reps each
Band dislocates: wide grip, pass band over head front-to-back. YTW: lying face down, raise arms in Y, T, W shapes. OG protocol for bulletproofing the shoulder girdle.

▼
EXERCISE
SETS × REPS
OG NOTES / ADVANCE WHEN
SKILL: Pistol Squat Progression
OG Skill — do first, fresh legs
3 × 3–5 / leg
Wk1-4: Sit to a low box, stand up one-legged. Wk5-8: Wall-assisted partial pistol. Wk9+: Full pistol. Your 195-lb squat means you have the strength — just learn the pattern. Rest 90s.
💪 STRENGTH A: Bulgarian Split Squat
Primary quad/glute builder — replaces leg press
4 × 5–8 / leg
Advance when: 3×8/leg with BW → add backpack weight → hold heaviest object available. OG principle: train in 5–8 rep range for strength. 2 min rest between legs. Best single-leg exercise in existence.
💪 STRENGTH B: Nordic Hamstring Curl
Replaces leg curl — OG considers this essential
3 × 3–6 negatives
Anchor feet under bed/couch. Tall-kneeling start. Lower yourself in 4–5 sec. Catch with hands, push back up. Advance when: 3×6 negatives → partial concentric → full curl. 2 min rest. Injury-prevention gold.
📦 ACCESSORY: Jump Squats
Power / RFD — OG power development
3 × 8–12 reps
Full depth squat, explode to jump. Land softly, reset. Replaces the power output of leg press. OG: power work after strength, not before. Crucial for hiking, running, and all sports. 60–90s rest.
📦 ACCESSORY: Single-Leg Calf Raise
Calves love daily high volume
3 × 20–25 / leg
On a step edge, full ROM down AND up. OG: calves are postural muscles — they handle huge volume. Do daily if possible. Essential for hiking and running injury prevention.
🩺 PREHAB: Hip 90/90 + SL RDL
Hip mobility + posterior chain
2 × 60s each side / 3 × 8 SL-RDL
Hip 90/90: sit with both knees at 90°, rotate between internal and external. SL-RDL: hinge on one leg, reaching opposite hand to floor. OG: hip mobility prevents most lower body issues in calisthenics.

Cycling (any terrain)
Cross-training, legs + aerobic, low impact
45–90 min

Legs + cardio — the whole point of sabbatical
2–8 hrs · any pace
OG: classify full-day hikes as a Lower Body + Cardio session — skip leg day the next day. Elevation gain = eccentric quad loading (hiking down = negative squat). Log distance + ascent to track load.

Full-body, zero-impact recovery + fitness
30–60 min
Pull strength (V-pull and H-pull) directly transfers to swimming. Kayaking = horizontal pull. OG: treat open water swimming as a separate skill domain worth developing. Great on hot days.
